Why Did I Switch Programs?

Chemical Engineering â†’ Computer Engineering

Initially, I chose Chem Eng because I was really good at high school chemistry and thought that I would like designing and controlling huge chemical processes. After taking CHE 100 (Chemical Engineering Concepts 1) I realized that Chemical Engineering is only about 30% chemistry and 70% process control. Needless to say, I did not do as well as I wanted in this course, but scored significantly higher in Calculus, Physics, and Linear Algebra. This was the first indication that the program was not meant for me.

I heard from a lot of upper years that the first job search will be hard regardless of what program you are in. I only got 1 interview in main round but luckily, I secured a placement 1 week before the end of classes. As a stream 4 student, I struggled a lot similar to my classmates to find a good releavant co-op placement in 1A and ended up as a Project Manager (PM) for a research company.

It was during my time as a PM that I got advice from my manager to switch programs. I really hated my first job and I was really scared of another irreleavant co-op placement in my future terms. I often got jealous of my friends who were in tech related fields enjoying their jobs and working on cool projects. I did not get treated very well by my boss and I took a leap of faith and switched programs as fast as possible.

Looking back now, Chem Eng is just a program that struggles finding good placements and requires a strong background for the first job search. I switched because I did not see myself doing this for the rest of my life and that I liked the computer engineering/computer science industry better.

This is not to say that Chem Eng is a horrible program and I am not trying to deter anyone from going into this field. This is my own experiences and opinions. I have a lot of good friends in Chem Eng that I am close to and they absolutely love the program. It just wasn't for me.

The Transfer Process

Information for Engineering Transfers

Initially, I emailed Engineer Transfers (email is found in the link above) and I attached a full page document on my reasons on transferring out. I wrote about what I disliked about the program and how I thought the program did not fit my personal goals.

When I sent the email, I initially applied to transfer into Mechanical Engineering. My marks were high enough (>80% or higher is the recommended average to switch engineering programs but that is subject to change based on every individual) but I was not able to transfer because the class size being too big.

When I got the denied, I was very upset. I knew personally that I could not pursue a field that I no longer had an interest in, so I asked to switch into Mechatronics Engineering. Again, I was denied due to the class size being too big. I was devasted and I knew I had to switch out but I was running out of choices.

At this point, I just applied to Computer Engineering because I wanted to do anything but Chem Eng. I did not really think it through or consult my parents. Luckily, they had space and I was admitted! I barely programmed in high school and I signed up for a program that required a lot of knowledge in computers/programming.

After I switched, all of my 1A credits were course equiavlent and I only had to take an online course COMP 206 at Athabasca University to make up for 1 credit. My original graduation date was not delayed.

I am very glad that it worked out because it turns out I really like this program and the course content matches what I want to do in the future. This was the best decision I made in my life. A word of advice to anyone reading: If you have any doubts about your program talk to your academic advisor. If you want to switch programs, DO IT AS SOON AS POSSIBLE AND DON'T WAIT. I have friends who wanted to switch out after 2A but it was incrediably difficult (They would graduate late or they did not have the marks to transfer out)
